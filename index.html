📚 Model Assumptions & Methodology

▼

5-Stage Funnel Model

  • Stage 1 - Reach: Total unique people exposed to brand campaign (1.2M reach at 9.4x frequency)
  • Stage 2 - Awareness: Percentage of reached audience who become aware of brand (attention rate × reach)
  • Stage 3 - Consideration: Aware audience who actively consider purchasing (driving site traffic and brand searches)
  • Stage 4 - Conversion: Consideration converts to customers via improved site CVR and sampling program
  • Stage 5 - Revenue Impact: New customers generate revenue through AOV, compounded by BTL halo effect

BTL Halo Effect

  • Brand campaigns typically improve performance channel efficiency by 10-75% (Binet & Field, IPA Databank (XXX update))
  • Applied to trackable BTL channels: Meta, Google, Email, Influencers
  • Halo effect represents brand awareness making paid channels more efficient
  • Not applied to Trade Marketing or WW Retail Media (no baseline ROAS data)

Attribution & Time Lag

  • Attribution Window: 4-month campaign period (May-Aug XXX)
  • Baseline Period: Historical performance before brand campaign
  • Long-term Value: Model shows immediate impact only; brand campaigns typically have 2-3 year tail effects
  • Incrementality: All incremental revenue represents lift above BTL-only baseline

Research Foundation

  • Binet & Field, IPA Databank (XXX update): "The Long and the Short of It" - Brand campaigns drive 10-75% performance uplift
  • Mark Ritson: Brand building compounds performance marketing effectiveness
  • IPA Databank: Integrated campaigns show higher ROI than performance-only approaches
  • Ehrenberg-Bass Institute: Mental availability drives category growth and conversion rates

Marketing Efficiency (%)

  • Represents incremental revenue returned for every $XM of total marketing spend.
  • For example, 75% means the campaign generates $XM in incremental revenue per $XM invested.
  • Used as a directional indicator of how effectively ATL + BTL activity is driving incremental commercial impact (not profit).

Funnel Conversion Benchmarks

  • Awareness Lift (35-75% typical): IPA Databank shows brand campaigns average 42-75% awareness lift. Nielsen Brand Effect studies show integrated campaigns drive 40-75% awareness lift with strong creative and media weight.
  • Consideration Rate (25-75% by category): Field & Binet research shows 20-75% of aware consumers actively consider. Health/wellness categories typically see 30-75% consideration rates (higher involvement, research-driven purchases). Premium/organic products: 40-75%.
  • Attention Rate (25-75%): System1 Group research shows campaigns with high creative quality achieve 40-75% attention rates. Average campaigns: 25-75%. Attention drives both awareness creation and consideration.
  • How to Adjust: Use brand tracking studies to measure your actual awareness and consideration rates. Update these inputs as campaign progresses to reflect real-world performance and improve forecast accuracy.
